First Friday: A Night of Art is a great opportunity to visit local galleries and businesses featuring rotating artwork. Held the first Friday of each month from 5-8 pm, it is a self-guided tour and maps are available at each of the participating galleries. Join us on June 2nd, 2017 for our next First Friday

### Joe Molina Gallery & Studios

930 8th Avenue

Join the Joe Molina Gallery and Studios in celebration of First Friday: A Night of Art from 5-8pm. The evening will feature Artist Liz Benfield and Musician Katie Hughes. Doors open at 5pm and entertainment begins at 6pm.

### Madison & Main Gallery

927 16th Street

The Madison and Main Gallery’s current show “Growth in Its Season” features thirteen guest artists and sixteen “Sprouting Artists”. On First Friday, June 2nd, from 5-8 pm, there will be an artist demonstration by Phil Chalk. He will explain the process of cold cast bronze. The demonstration will be at the Gallery and this is a demonstration not to miss. The slate of guest artists for this show are: Jaz Allmer, Mary Benke, Phil Chalk, Jim Digby, Sean Flanagan, Adele McKenna, Johanna Mueller, Sylvia Ramirez, Jana Ramseier, Pat Seburn, Lynn Shoup, Connie Stewart, and Irene Vialpando. The “Sprouting Artists” from Aims Community College advanced painting students are: Hannah Cairns, Emily Hopwood, Nichol Marquez, Kathryn Norris, Melissa Pedersen, Philana Quick, Marilee Smith, Alexa Tabor, Becky Safarik, Susan Safarik, and Amy Stuart. The University of Northern Colorado ceramics students are: Marissa Gurule, Hannah Hughes, Crystal Musser, Dana O'Toole, and Lili Silva. The 30<sup>th</sup> annual Garden Show runs through June 30, 2017. The Madison & Main is an artists-owned gallery. Hours are 10am-6pm. Monday through Friday and 10am-4pm Saturday. For more details about the show, call the Madison & Main Gallery at (970) 351-6201 or visit [www.madisonandmaingallery.com](http://www.madisonandmaingallery.com/)

### The Dragon’s Cache

1109 7th Street

The Dragon’s Cache will feature Indigo Love Art created by Keila (Ksea) and Deandra (Deevine). Indigo Love’s main focus is creating eco art while connecting the beautiful web of life through all of its strains and inclusiveness. They recycle items like cardboard and wood to use as canvases with hopes to inspire and shift even if in small ways the way others invest, upcycle, reuse and waste. Refreshments served.
